# ACP (Agent communication protocol)

**ACP** (Agent Communication Protocol) differs from **A2A** (Agent-to-Agent) in that ACP is designed for **packaging, discovering, and remotely executing agents** across platforms, while A2A focuses on **asynchronous messaging and coordination** between already-running agents. You’d use **ACP** when you want to **deploy portable agents**, enable **cross-framework interoperability**, or **run agents on-demand** in distributed environments. In contrast, use **A2A** when agents are already active and need to **collaborate or delegate tasks** in real time or asynchronously. ACP is more about **agent lifecycle and portability**, while A2A is about **communication and coordination**.
